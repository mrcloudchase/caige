The Role
What is a Guardrail Engineer?

AI guardrails prevent AI systems from being used in ways they shouldn't. They enforce boundaries around what models can say, do, and access — spanning content safety, security, and operational reliability.

A guardrail engineer works across all three of these pillars, designing and maintaining the systems that keep AI safe and trustworthy in production. It is a discipline that combines security engineering, ML operations, and policy expertise into a single, critical function.

Three Pillars of AI Guardrails

  • Content Safety — Preventing harmful, biased, or policy-violating outputs from reaching users
  • Security — Defending against prompt injection, data exfiltration, and adversarial attacks
  • Operational Safety — Ensuring reliability, observability, and graceful failure in production systems
